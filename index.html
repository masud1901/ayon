About Me

I am a Machine Learning researcher at the Institute of Information Technology, Jahangirnagar University, with expertise in computer vision and deep learning. My research journey began with my thesis on mosquito detection using deep learning (MosQNet-SA) under Dr. Shahidul Islam. Currently, I am working with Dr. Mohammad Abu Yousuf on projects involving ECG signal analysis, Alzheimer’s detection, and steganography, in collaboration with Md. Biplob Hossen.

I explored quantum machine learning through a collaboration with Md. Abrar Jahin, leading to projects like KACQ-DCNN, a quantum-classical hybrid for heart disease detection, and quantum neural networks for particle physics. These experiences inspired my focus on self-supervised learning, which now drives much of my work.

Beyond research, I previously served as an Executive at the E-Business & Entrepreneurship Club (EEC-JU) and as Media & Communications Lead at the IEEE Student Branch. Currently, I lead the IIT-JU Sports Club and actively participate in athletics.

My passion lies in creating machine learning systems that thrive on limited labeled data, addressing challenges in critical areas like healthcare and physics.
